Apply on Hindus first: Opposition slams PM Modi’s Uniform Civil Code pitch

Hours after Prime Minister Narendra Modi batted for the Uniform Civil Code (UCC) pitch while addressing BJP workers in poll-bound Madhya Pradesh, Opposition parties slammed him for his remarks and called it an attack on “India’s pluralism and diversity”.By India Today News Desk: Hours after Prime Minister Narendra Modi made a pitch for the Uniform Civil Code (UCC) and lashed out at the opposition for playing "vote bank politics"

Attacking the Opposition while addressing BJP workers under the ‘Mera Booth Sabse Majboot’ campaign in poll-bound Madhya Pradesh on Tuesday, PM Modi said the people are being instigated in the name of the Uniform Civil Code (UCC). He also asked how the country can function with dual laws that govern personal matters, and accused the Opposition of using the UCC issue to "mislead and provoke" the Muslim community.

PM Modi said, “Will a family function if there are two different sets of rules for people? Then how will a country run? Our Constitution too guarantees equal rights to all people.”"India's PM considers India's diversity and its pluralism a problem.
